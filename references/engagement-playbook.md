# Engagement Playbook — Maximizing Post Reach on LinkedIn

A great post with zero engagement strategy will underperform a good post with disciplined follow-through. LinkedIn's algorithm heavily rewards early engagement velocity — the first 60 minutes determine whether your post reaches hundreds or thousands.

---

## Pre-Posting Prep (10 minutes before publishing)

### Draft 2-3 reply templates for likely comments
Before you post, predict the 2-3 most likely reactions and draft substantive replies. This lets you respond instantly instead of composing on the fly.

Example predictions:
- **Agreement comment** ("So true, I've seen this too") — Reply draft that asks a specific follow-up question
- **Pushback comment** ("But what about [exception]?") — Reply draft that acknowledges the exception and adds nuance
- **Question comment** ("How do you recommend doing X?") — Reply draft with a concise 2-3 step answer

### Open LinkedIn on phone AND desktop
You need both. Phone for quick replies when away from desk. Desktop for longer, more thoughtful responses and for monitoring analytics.

### Block 60 minutes after posting
Treat this like a meeting. Do not schedule calls, deep work, or anything that takes you away from your post during the critical first hour. If you can't protect 60 minutes, delay the post until you can.

### Prepare the first comment
Write your first comment BEFORE posting. It should contain:
- Links to sources mentioned in the post (LinkedIn suppresses posts with links in the body)
- Additional context that didn't fit in the main post
- A call to engagement: "Which of these resonated most with you?" or "What would you add to this list?"

Save it in your notes app, ready to paste immediately after publishing.

## First 60 Minutes — The Critical Window

This is when LinkedIn's algorithm decides whether to show your post to 500 people or 50,000. Every action here is high-leverage.

### Immediately after posting (0-2 minutes)
1. **Paste your first comment.** Do this within 60 seconds of posting. The first comment with links, sources, or bonus context signals to the algorithm that the post is generating activity.
2. **Do NOT edit the post.** Editing within the first hour can reset algorithmic distribution. If you spot a typo, leave it unless it changes the meaning. (Yes, really.)

### Active monitoring (2-60 minutes)
3. **Reply to EVERY comment within 5 minutes.** Speed matters more than perfection here. LinkedIn's algorithm counts comment-reply pairs as high-quality engagement signals.

4. **Make replies substantive (3+ sentences minimum).** Bad replies kill momentum. Never reply with just:
   - "Thanks!"
   - "Appreciate it!"
   - "Great point!"
   - Any single emoji

   Instead, every reply should do at least one of these:
   - **Add new information** the commenter didn't have
   - **Ask a follow-up question** that invites them to respond again
   - **Share a related experience** that extends the conversation
   - **Respectfully challenge** something they said (this creates thread depth)

   **Example of a bad reply:**
   > "Thanks for sharing, great insight!"

   **Example of a good reply:**
   > "That's a really interesting point about onboarding. We actually tested two approaches last quarter — a self-serve walkthrough vs. a 15-min guided call. The guided call had 3x better retention at day 30, but it obviously doesn't scale the same way. Have you found a middle ground that works?"

5. **Ask follow-up questions in replies.** This is the single highest-leverage engagement tactic. A question in your reply turns a 1-comment interaction into a 3-4 comment thread. LinkedIn counts thread depth heavily.

   Strong follow-up question templates:
   - "Curious — have you experienced that in [their industry] specifically?"
   - "What's been your biggest challenge with that approach?"
   - "How did your team react when you tried that?"
   - "What would you change if you could start over?"

6. **Like every comment.** This is the bare minimum. Even if you can't reply immediately, like it. The commenter gets a notification, which brings them back to the post.

7. **Pin the best comment.** Once you've seen 5-10 comments, pin the one that is most insightful, most controversial, or most likely to generate further discussion. Pinned comments appear first and set the tone for the conversation.

## Hours 2-24 — Sustained Momentum

The algorithm continues evaluating engagement velocity for up to 24 hours. Your job shifts from "respond to everything instantly" to "keep the conversation alive."

### Every 2 hours: check and reply
- Set a phone timer or calendar reminder
- Reply to all new comments (still aim for substantive replies, but 2+ sentences is acceptable here)
- If a thread is getting deep (4+ replies), let it breathe — don't force more replies where the conversation has naturally concluded

### Share strategically (hours 2-6)
- Share the post to 1-2 relevant LinkedIn groups where you're an active member (not a drive-by poster)
- Do NOT share to more than 2 groups — it looks spammy and some groups will flag you
- When sharing to a group, add a sentence of context: "Wrote about X — curious if anyone here has seen the same trend"

### DM follow-ups (hours 4-12)
- Send personal DMs to 3-5 people who left thoughtful comments
- DO NOT send copy-paste messages. Reference their specific comment:
  > "Loved your take on [specific thing they said] — curious, have you experienced [related question]? Would love to hear more about your approach."
- This builds genuine relationships AND signals to LinkedIn that your content generates real connections
- Do NOT DM people who only liked (no comment) — that feels intrusive

### Acknowledge shares
- If someone reposts your content, thank them publicly in a comment on their share
- This is visible to THEIR network, which expands your reach to a new audience

## Post-Post Analysis (24-48 hours after publishing)

Track these metrics for every post. Without data, you're guessing. With data, you're iterating.

### Metrics to record

| Metric | Where to find it | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Impressions | Post analytics | Raw reach — how many eyeballs |
| Engagement rate | (reactions + comments + shares) / impressions | Quality of reach — did people care? |
| Comments (total) | Post analytics | Algorithm signal + conversation quality |
| Profile views (24h after) | LinkedIn dashboard | Did the post make people curious about YOU? |
| Connection requests (24h after) | LinkedIn notifications | Strongest signal — people want more of your content |
| Follower growth (24h after) | LinkedIn dashboard | Long-term audience building |

### Analysis questions to answer after every post

1. **Which hook type did I use?** (Stat / Contrarian / Story / Question / Curiosity Gap)
2. **What was the impressions-to-comment ratio?** (Good: >1% comments/impressions. Great: >2%)
3. **Which comments generated the deepest threads?** (What topic within the post sparked the most discussion?)
4. **Did any comment add a perspective I hadn't considered?** (Use this in future posts — your audience is telling you what they care about)
5. **Who were the top 3 engagers?** (These are potential collaborators, podcast guests, co-authors, or future clients)
6. **What time did I post, and how did the first-hour engagement compare to previous posts?** (Build a posting time dataset over weeks)

### Record format

Keep a simple spreadsheet or note with one row per post:

```
Date | Hook Type | Topic | Impressions | Comments | Profile Views | Connection Reqs | Notes
```

After 10 posts, patterns will emerge. After 20, you'll know your audience.

## Daily Engagement Habit (10 minutes/day, every day)

This is non-negotiable. You cannot post once a week and ignore LinkedIn the other 6 days. The algorithm rewards consistent daily activity, and your audience grows through reciprocal engagement.

### Comment on 3-5 posts per day in your niche

**What counts as a good comment:**
- Adds a perspective or personal experience (3+ sentences)
- Asks a smart follow-up question
- Respectfully offers an alternative viewpoint
- Shares a relevant data point or resource

**What does NOT count:**
- "Great post!" or any 1-2 word reply
- Generic emoji reactions
- Copy-paste comments you use on multiple posts
- Anything that could apply to any post on any topic

### Target the right people
- **Sweet spot: 1K-50K followers.** These creators are large enough to have an active audience but small enough to notice and reciprocate your engagement.
- **Avoid: 100K+ followers.** Your comment will be buried among hundreds. Low ROI on your time.
- **Avoid: <500 followers with low engagement.** Not enough audience to make reciprocation valuable.

### The reciprocation engine
When you consistently leave thoughtful comments on someone's posts:
1. They notice you (within 1-2 weeks of consistent commenting)
2. They check your profile and see your content
3. They start engaging with YOUR posts
4. Their audience sees your comments and checks you out
5. Some of their audience follows you

This is how LinkedIn audiences grow organically. Posting alone is half the equation. Engaging is the other half.

## Posting Cadence Guidelines

| Cadence | Pros | Cons | Recommended for |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily (5-7x/week) | Maximum visibility, fast growth | High burnout risk, quality can drop | Full-time creators |
| 3x/week | Strong growth, sustainable | Need high quality per post | Most professionals |
| 2x/week | Sustainable, allows for deep research | Slower growth | Busy professionals with demanding jobs |
| 1x/week | Minimal time commitment | Very slow growth, algorithm deprioritizes | Absolute minimum — not recommended long-term |

**Recommended starting cadence: 2x per week** with the goal of moving to 3x within a month as the writing process becomes faster.

**Best posting times (general — test for your audience):**
- Tuesday-Thursday: 7:30-8:30 AM local time of your primary audience
- Tuesday-Thursday: 12:00-1:00 PM (lunch scroll)
- Avoid: Friday afternoon, weekends, Monday morning (people are catching up)

## Rules That Override Everything

1. **Never buy engagement.** Pods, engagement groups, and paid likes destroy your audience quality and LinkedIn can detect them.
2. **Never post and disappear.** A post with zero author replies in the first hour signals to the algorithm that you don't care. It will reflect that in your reach.
3. **Never edit in the first hour.** Resets distribution. Accept the typo.
4. **Never put links in the post body.** Always move links to the first comment. LinkedIn suppresses posts with outbound links.
5. **Never argue in comments.** Disagree respectfully, add nuance, ask questions. The moment you get defensive, you've lost the audience.
6. **Consistency beats virality.** One viral post means nothing if you disappear for 2 weeks after. Steady 2-3x/week posting with engagement will outperform sporadic brilliance every time.
